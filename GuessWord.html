Why

GuessWord is obviously inspired by Wordle. My partner in life was concerned that the NYT might put her favorite word game behind a pay-wall, so she asked me to write something like it just for her. As a retired software developer, and somewhat rusty, I took up the challenge.

I used W3.CSS, CSS, Javascript, jQuery and HTML, edited using Visual Studio 2022. I also created different dictionaries from those used in Wordle. The Wordle dictionary and solutions are in plain text and easy to find but I decided to use a dictionary based on TWL06 and a solution set based on various common word lists.

How to play

Your task is to deduce the target word that GuessWord has randomly selected from its dictionary of common words. You can enter your guess using the on-screen keyboard or a physical keyboard, if available. When you select "enter", GuessWord colors each letter in your guess using the following rules:
  1. If the letter is not present in the target word, it is colored grey.
  2. If the letter is present in the target word but is in the wrong position, it is colored yellow.
  3. If the letter is present and in the right position, it is colored green.
  4. If the guess is not in the GuessWord dictionary of almost all words, then all the letters in the guess are colored red. You can use the backspace key (⌫) to clear the guess. Alternatively, you can click or tap letters in the word and change them using either keyboard. When you do this, the letter you tap is highlit with a red dashed border. After you enter a letter or simply click or tap the highlit cell, the border is cleared. If the word is complete and you correct it, Guessword will remove the red, but only if the word is in its full dictionary.
  5. If your guess contains a letter that appears more than once in the target, then only a letter in the correct position will be colored green. Otherwise, the first match in the target will be colored yellow.
  6. If your guess contains the same letter more than once, then only a letter in the correct position will be colored green. Otherwise, the first match in the guess will be colored yellow.
Once you have completed a word, but before you have entered it, you can click on any letter(s) in the word and change it. This avoids you having to backspace out a typo. Sometimes, you end up with too many possibilities and too few turns to try them all. Suppose you have "WH--E". You choices are "WHALE", "WHERE", "WHILE", "WHINE", "WHITE", "WHOLE". "WHORE" and "WHOSE". In this case you could try a guess like "RIOTS". Depending on which letters are flagged green or yellow, you can determine which words can be removed fron the list of possibilities.

Special Features

  1. GuessWord lets you choose between 4, 5 or 6 letter word games. Just click the "Games" menu item to choose.
  2. At the end of a game, you can start a new game by clicking the "Play" menu item or by clicking or pressing the enter key.
  3. GuessWord records your results in local storage inside you browser's memory. This information is used to show you your history when you select "history" from the menu.
  4. GuessWord does not use the Wordle word list or dictionary. Some words, like "SOARE" and "ROATE" do not work in GuessWord. Obvious obscenities have been deleted.
  5. GuessWord is completely browser based. The only time it communicates with the host web server is when you open up the GuessWord web page. After that, there is no further communication with the server. You can play or continue playing even if you don’t have an internet cnnection provided you started Griddle while you had a connection.
